Post 2 - Article HTML

Working With Semantic Markup (HTML) and Style Sheets (CSS)

October 28th 2025

Ever wonder how to create effective semantic markup? After reading the article “What is Semantic HTML? And How to Use it Correctly” [website], I decided to write about how I used semantic HTML in my article and how that differed from the style sheet that I used to create a cohesive visual design.

In my styled article I used HTML to format the article into different sections based on content. For example I used the “header” and the “article” tags to differentiate the difference between the title and the main portion of my article.

I do believe that the more correct tag of the semantic markup would be to use the “main” tag for the main section of the article and then use the section tags to differentiate between the references and the Appendix (which could be listed in an “aside” tag). I also believe that the Table of Contents could be listed in the “nav” tags which would help search engines and screen readers access my content more easily.

I used hierarchical headings to list content to what the information is related to. For example, in the content “Hong Kong and China” [website] is listed in an H3 tag which means that that content is a subcategory to the above h2 heading “STEM and Design Education around the Globe” and the h1 title listed at the top of the article.

In regards to styling I was trying to keep it simple, something that would reflect an academic article. One thing specifically challenging visually was making the block quotes stand out but not overwhelming the viewer. I made the font size bigger and chose a cool chromatic gray to dampen and balance the text on screen.

Overall I had a lot of fun working with semantic markup and style sheets and I look forward to when I use HTML and CSS in the future!
