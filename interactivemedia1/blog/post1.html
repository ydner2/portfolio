Digital Transformation and the Spread of Misinformation

October 21st 2025

Every day millions of people use the internet for work, to shop, and to learn about new things. Much of what I do for work–a blog writer that focuses on building SEO pages, graphic design work, and even UX/UI design–is directly influenced and heavily impacted by the internet and the new innovations such as Social Media and Generative AI inquiries.

Because information is readily available for work, leisure, and yes… even life advice! It presents a problem of misinformation. Social Media is a fast way to get information immediately and some platforms even present new information that is deemed “socially acceptable” and NEW or novice. When thinking about AI, you could imagine speedy gonzales from Looney Tunes, a fast way of gaining information and results.

Because results and information are so fast and immediate this can present problems for the general public of accessing accurate information. For example, an academic article mentioned that social media misinformation can directly affect health decision choices such as choosing not to vaccinate [website]. Another example is through the spread of Disinformation in regards to political figures. Did you know that Donald Trump was deepfaked to show that he was hugging Anthony Fauci [website]. This presents a growing concern is the amount of information that is available and the impact this has on society.

In my own line of work I also do social media work. Often, I have to bridge the gap between local SEO, popularity and trends, and branding in a dog company. In fact, my employer is very specific in the way that pictures are taken so that it does not convey the wrong idea and release misinformation. Thus I am required to take time to carefully analyze dog behavior in order to post to Social Media. I guess the job description increases with Social Media managing!
